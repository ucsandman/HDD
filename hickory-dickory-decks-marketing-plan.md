## Part 5: Seasonal Marketing Strategy

### Understanding HDD's Seasonal Business

| Season | Lead Volume | Strategy |
|--------|-------------|----------|
| Winter (Dec-Feb) | Low | Plant seeds, nurture, book spring projects |
| Spring (Mar-May) | Peak | All systems go, maximize conversions |
| Summer (Jun-Aug) | High | Continue momentum, manage capacity |
| Fall (Sep-Nov) | Moderate | Off-season discounts, capture "next year" leads |

### Seasonal Content & Offers

**Winter Campaign: "Plan Now, Build in Spring"**
- Email nurture sequence to past inquiries
- "Beat the spring rush" booking incentive
- Educational content about planning process
- Design consultations with spring build dates

**Spring Campaign: "Your Deck Awaits"**
- Aggressive Google Ads spend
- Review request push from previous year customers
- Before/after content heavy
- Limited availability messaging

**Summer Campaign: "Still Time This Season"**
- Target late deciders
- Highlight quick turnaround
- Pool deck and hot tub focus (summer enjoyment)

**Fall Campaign: "Off-Season Advantage"**
- Promote fall building benefits (cooler weather, better scheduling)
- Pricing incentives
- "Book now, enjoy next spring" messaging

## Key Takeaways for HDD

1. **Reviews are your moat.** Your 4.8-4.9 ratings are a massive competitive advantage. Systematize review collection and protect that rating with sentiment routing.

2. **Speed wins in home services.** First to respond gets the job 78% of the time. Automate the first 5 minutes.

3. **Seasonal strategy matters.** Build leads in winter for spring projects. Don't go dark in off-season.

4. **Local SEO is everything.** For a franchise model, each location needs optimized GBP, local landing pages, and location-specific content.

5. **Content compounds.** One blog post ranking for "deck builders [city]" can generate leads for years.

6. **Own your system.** Building internal automation capability is more valuable than outsourcing to agencies long-term.
